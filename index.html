Where to begin · Zero to value hunter

  • 2–4 weeks (free) – Zerodha Varsity: “Intro to Stock Markets” + “Fundamental Analysis” modules. Demat, ratios, intrinsic value.
  • Infrastructure (1 weekend) – Open demat with any discount broker of your choice. Link Aadhaar, PAN, bank account. ₹10L in savings/liquid fund – do NOT invest before Varsity + screening 100+ companies.
  • Full-time research mindset – 2–4 hours daily reading reports, screening small caps. With ₹10L, take small positions (₹20k–50k) across 20–40 ideas.
    • “Be in love with the subject” – Buffett. Treat research like a job.

    Ideal criteria (Buffett edge)
  • Mkt cap < ₹2,000–5,000 Cr (ideally under ₹1,000 Cr)
  • Revenue/earnings CAGR >15% (3–5 years)
  • ROCE/ROE >15–20% · low/zero debt
  • Promoter holding >50% · clean governance
  • Niche businesses: specialty chemicals, auto ancillaries, railways, packaging, small manufacturing
    • Avoid: Penny stocks, high debt, frequent dilution, related-party red flags, social media hypes.
    “Green Bay & Western Railroad” equivalents – small, boring, mispriced Indian gems.

    Reality check · risk & returns

  • No guaranteed 50% annual return – Buffett style demands passion & skill. Small/micro caps offer high potential but carry permanent capital loss risk due to illiquidity, fraud, volatility.
  • Start small, continuous learning, never invest money you cannot afford to lose.
  • “The key is consistency, patience, and genuine curiosity about every small company.”
    • ⚡ High risk, high research reward. Big funds ignore these names – your edge is deep work.
